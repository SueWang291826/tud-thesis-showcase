# Emergency Procedures and Safety Guide

## Fire / Smoke Emergency

1. **Do not use elevators** during a fire alarm. Use only escalators and stairs.
2. Follow illuminated EXIT signs on Level F3 toward Gates A–D.
3. From Level F1 (Platform): proceed to Level F3 via the nearest staircase (Stair S1 north end, Stair S2 south end).
4. Assembly points are located outside Gate A (east plaza) and Gate D (west plaza).
5. Station loudspeakers will provide guidance. Follow staff instructions at all times.

## Evacuation Routes by Level

| Level | Primary exit | Backup exit |
|---|---|---|
| F1 Platform | Stair S1 → F3 → Gate A/B/C | Stair S2 → F3 → Gate D |
| F3 Concourse | Gate D (east) | Gate A, B, or C (via F4) |
| F4 Transport Hub | Direct exit via Gates A, B, C | Escalator E3 down to F3 → Gate D |

## Power Failure

Emergency lighting activates automatically. DO NOT panic. Remain in place unless directed otherwise. Staff will direct passengers to exits within 2 minutes.

## Medical Emergency

- **Emergency intercoms** (red button) are located on every platform door and inside all elevators.
- First aid kits are posted at the Level F3 Gate D security desk.
- AED (Automated External Defibrillator) units are at: F3 west toilet block corridor and F4 Gate B service counter.
- Call 120 (ambulance) or press the platform intercom. Staff response time is under 3 minutes.

## Suspicious Package / Security Threat

1. Do NOT touch or approach the object.
2. Alert staff immediately by pressing any intercom or approaching the nearest staff member.
3. Follow evacuation instructions if announced.
4. Security screening (X-ray) is conducted at all fare gate entrances.

## Lost Child

Report to the nearest staff member or security desk (F3 near Gate D). The child will be taken to the Customer Service Counter (F3, Gate D). Station CCTV can assist in locating lost family members.

## Train Stuck Between Stations

Stay calm, remain seated, and follow crew instructions. Emergency doors are located at both ends of each carriage. Walk-through to adjacent carriages is possible in emergencies.
